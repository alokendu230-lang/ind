3 .MAC(MEDIA ACCESS CONTROL) ADDRESS


  • MAC Address Meaning

A MAC (Media Access Control) Address is a special ID given to every device that connects to a network, like your phone, laptop, smart TV, or Wi-Fi router. It helps identify the device on a network, just like a house address identifies a home.


  • Permanent Number

A MAC address is built into the hardware at the factory. It does not change, even if you restart your device or change the IP address.

  • 48-Bit Number

    • MAC addresses are 48 bits long in computer language (binary). When converted for humans to read, it becomes 12 characters made up of numbers and letters.

    • Written in Hexadecimal

    MAC addresses are 48 bits long in computer language (binary). When converted for humans to read, it becomes 12 characters made up of numbers and letters.

    • Colon Format (Most Common Style)

    The most popular and widely used format for MAC addresses. The 12 characters are grouped into six pairs separated by colons (:). This is what you’ll most likely see on smartphones, laptops, or Wi-Fi settings.
    Example: 3C:22:FB:A3:B4:C5

    • Hyphen Format (Common on Windows)

    Windows computers often display MAC addresses with hyphens (-) instead of colons. It works the same way — only the separator changes.
    Example: 3C-22-FB-A3-B4-C5

    • Period Format (Used by Cisco Devices)

    Some network devices, especially Cisco routers and switches, display MAC addresses in a period-separated format. Instead of six pairs, they group the characters into three sets of four characters separated by periods.
    Example: 3c22.fba3.b4c5

    • No Separator Format (Less Common)

    No Separator Format (Less Common) In some software or technical logs, the MAC address may appear without any separators. This is less readable but still valid.
    Example: 3C22FBA3B4C5
